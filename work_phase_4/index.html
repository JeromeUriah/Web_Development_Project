IMPLEMENTATION RATIONALE

Landing Page

Alignment with Client and User Needs

The landing page has been designed to directly reflect the goals outlined by the Connect Communities Initiative (CCI), particularly in providing a user-friendly, inclusive space that supports exploration, discovery, and community connection. By clearly segmenting each major function—Shared Space, Events Hub, Resource Hub, User Wall, and Community Hub. The landing page empowers users to understand the platform’s structure at a glance, which is especially important for users unfamiliar with digital tools or those relying on assistive technologies. The page aligns with user feedback and design research from earlier phases that emphasized the need for clarity, minimal cognitive load, and immediate visibility of value. It supports both key audiences identified in the brief: tech and innovation community members looking for interaction, and general users who want to learn more about the community. The inclusion of descriptive headings, thematic icons, and concise explanations ensures that the content is not only easy to locate but also resonates with user interests and expectations, thus enabling intuitive navigation and supporting the client's aim to increase engagement and participation across all demographics. A standout feature supporting these aims is the Daily Trending carousel, which offers a real-time preview of the most active or discussed posts. This directly responds to user desires for immediacy and relevance, promoting timely engagement while showcasing community voices. By surfacing fresh, topical content in a compact and interactive format, the carousel encourages repeat visits and enhances visibility for user-generated contributions—both of which are central to CCI’s mission of digital empowerment and social cohesion.

Visual and Structural Design Coherence

The visual design maintains consistency across the platform, with uniform iconography, layout grids, typographic hierarchy, and thematic illustrations that match the visual language established in the broader site. The page structure is linear and scrollable, with clear dividers and ARIA-labelled headings that align with the site’s overall mobile-first and content-forward design principles. The cohesive visual flow reinforces usability and ensures familiarity across sections while maintaining aesthetic unity with the design proposal.

Accessibility and Ethical Considerations

Accessibility is embedded through semantic HTML, proper ARIA labels for interactive elements, and alt text for all informative images. Ethical design principles are applied by ensuring that all content is inclusive, non-commercial, and culturally sensitive, in alignment with CCI’s brief. Placeholder and non-critical content is marked accordingly, and no features depend on colour alone, supporting users with various needs including visual and cognitive impairments.

Design Decisions and Refinements

While the structure remained faithful to the A3 proposal, the integration of ARIA roles and improved alt text were refinements made during implementation to boost screen reader compatibility. Additionally, headings and image captions were revised to enhance clarity and convey information contextually for assistive technology. These decisions were guided by user testing feedback and a deeper review of WCAG 2.2 during the build process

Accessibility Audit Summary

The IBM Equal Access Accessibility Checker was run on the deployed landing page, yielding a clean report with no critical or serious violations. Minor recommendations included verifying contrast for placeholder slides, which were acknowledged but deemed low-priority due to their temporary status. The audit validated strong compliance with WCAG 2.2 Level AA, confirming that the implementation meets the required accessibility standards for a diverse and inclusive user base.

Shared Space

Alignment with Client and User Needs

The Shared Space page is central to fulfilling the Connect Communities Initiative (CCI)’s objective of creating an inclusive, participatory platform that empowers users to express themselves and engage meaningfully with one another. It provides a digital environment where users can post ideas, share opinions, and respond to each other—core community-building interactions identified in both the client brief and earlier user research. The intuitive “Add New Post” field supports quick, low-barrier content creation, while topic filters like “General,” “AI,” and “Computing” allow users to easily explore content that matches their interests. Posts feature visible metadata (author, date, topic) and interactive buttons for liking, commenting, sharing, and reporting, supporting both engagement and moderation needs. These features reflect users’ desires for real-time interaction, identity visibility, and social validation, while the platform ensures safety through clear reporting pathways. For new users, the onboarding experience is reinforced by the embedded “Community Sign-Up” form, making the transition from observer to contributor seamless. Altogether, the Shared Space page actively promotes the goals of user connection, free expression, and accessible participation in the tech and innovation community, delivering on both functional and emotional user needs in alignment with CCI’s mission. Additionally, the inclusion of the “Events Happening Soon” section responds to user expectations for up-to-date, actionable content that encourages offline or real-time engagement. By previewing a featured upcoming event with a clear title, time, and location, the section ensures visibility for community-driven activities and encourages planning and participation—especially for users who prefer content feeds over calendar views. This integration reflects the client's goal of supporting community cohesion through active involvement and access to shared experiences.

Visual and Structural Design Coherence

The Shared Space page maintains consistent visual styling with the rest of the platform through structured containers, typographic uniformity, and iconography standards. Post cards, buttons, and the sidebar follow the same spacing and layout conventions used site-wide. The “Events Happening Soon” section mirrors these visual principles with a clean, bordered layout and balanced text-image pairing. Headings are styled consistently, tags are color-coded for easy identification, and the grid-based layout ensures content aligns evenly across both desktop and mobile displays. These coherent design elements help create a seamless user experience and reinforce site identity.

Accessibility and Ethical Considerations

Accessibility features have been implemented throughout, including properly paired labels in the form section, descriptive ARIA labels on interactive buttons, and semantic ordering of post content to support screen readers. The “Events Happening Soon” section uses structured date and time formats and alt text for all images, ensuring screen reader compatibility and cognitive clarity. The form also uses fieldsets for logical grouping. Ethically, the platform avoids commercial influence, promotes safe discourse through a report mechanism, and offers an inclusive space that upholds CCI’s values of representation, safety, and respectful engagement

Design Decisions and Refinements

Refinements made during implementation included enhancing the visibility and intuitiveness of the “Add New Post” prompt, improving topic filtering alignment, and optimizing content padding for legibility. The “Events Happening Soon” block was placed in close proximity to the sign-up form, based on feedback that users engage more when information is spatially contextualized. To support this, the layout was simplified to highlight only key event details and reduce visual noise, while maintaining alignment with the broader site design. Button focus states and ARIA labels were added manually to ensure accessibility and improved user interaction

Accessibility Audit Summary

The IBM Equal Access Accessibility Checker confirmed that the Shared Space page complies with WCAG 2.2 Level AA standards. Key accessibility checkpoints such as alt text, semantic structure, and interactive focus states were validated. The “Events Happening Soon” component also passed without issue, with the only minor refinement being the addition of descriptive alt text to the event image. Overall, the audit verified that all users—including those using assistive technology—can interact with the page in an inclusive and seamless manner.

Events Page

Alignment with Client and User Needs

The Events Hub page directly fulfills the Connect Communities Initiative (CCI)’s goals of promoting meaningful community engagement through event-based participation. It provides a central space for community members to both create and discover events, addressing the client’s call for dynamic content that encourages real-world or virtual connection. The event creation form is designed to be approachable and complete, empowering users with various technical backgrounds to submit meaningful event listings, including title, date, time, location, and a short description. These input fields reflect user needs for clarity and control, and the form’s straightforward structure lowers barriers to contribution. The page also includes a live-generated event listing area (“Created Events!”), which reinforces a sense of user ownership and real-time feedback. Most significantly, the inclusion of the “Events Happening Soon” section in the right panel echoes insights from earlier design research: users value timely, actionable content they don’t have to search for. By surfacing featured events alongside the form and sign-up prompt, the layout reinforces the importance of connection and immediacy. It ensures that users—whether browsing casually or deeply involved—are consistently made aware of opportunities to participate. The result is a tool that both informs and empowers, aligning tightly with the client’s objective to support social cohesion and digital community development through inclusive, accessible event management.

Visual and Structural Design Coherence

The Events Hub continues the visual identity of the platform with consistent typography, button design, and spacing conventions. Each section is visually segmented using dividers and fieldsets that guide the eye logically through creation, discovery, and participation. The form and listings use matching margin structures and card-based layouts, and the events preview card in the sidebar reflects the visual language of similar components in Shared Space and the Landing Page. The double-column layout efficiently balances form functionality with community insight, giving equal weight to creation and exploration while maintaining a mobile-responsive structure. Overall, the structural rhythm and aesthetic uniformity help reduce user friction and encourage confident navigation.

Accessibility and Ethical Considerations

Design refinements included grouping related fields (e.g., event type, organiser, and time/location) into logical chunks, improving usability and reducing cognitive load. Input field spacing was increased for clarity, and feedback areas were added to provide success/error messages during submission. The preview card in the right panel was simplified to emphasize time-sensitive event details without overwhelming users. Responsive styles were tuned to ensure that form fields remained legible and tappable on smaller screens. The design was also iterated to reduce visual competition between the form and dynamic content area, creating a more balanced and engaging layout.

Design Decisions and Refinements

The initial design was refined to reduce friction and encourage participation: originally split across multiple pages, the form and event list were merged into a single, scrollable interface to create a sense of immediacy and control. The form fields were reorganised for logical progression, and text descriptions were tightened for clarity. Placeholder elements were added for future scalability, including photo previews. Button size and spacing were increased for touch accessibility, and layout responsiveness was verified across major devices. Refinements also included reducing cognitive overload by limiting the visual density of event previews and form instructions.

Accessibility Audit Summary

The IBM Equal Access Accessibility Checker confirmed that the Events Hub page complies with WCAG 2.2 Level AA. No critical or serious issues were detected. Minor recommendations, such as clarifying the alt text for the preview image and ensuring the form's fieldset nesting order, were addressed through HTML updates. ARIA roles were verified, and all interactive elements were tested for keyboard access and logical focus order. The event date-time values were confirmed to be screen-reader-friendly. Overall, the audit validated that the page supports a broad range of users and maintains ethical, inclusive accessibility practices.

Community Hub

Alignment with Client and User Needs

The Community Hub page was designed to embody the Connect Communities Initiative (CCI)’s core mission of fostering safe, welcoming, and participatory digital environments for diverse community groups. According to the client brief, the site needed to facilitate visibility, engagement, and ethical participation. This page delivers on those expectations by centralising access to foundational community elements: guidelines, members, announcements, and sign-up—all accessible via visually distinctive, clearly labeled cards. From a visceral perspective, the design evokes approachability and trust through large headings, warm card layouts, and the inclusion of a welcome form paired with community values. Behaviourally, users can easily scan and click through to learn the rules, view participants, or stay updated—all of which support confident, self-guided participation. Reflectively, the act of joining the community through a simple form—paired with real-time events and community values—reinforces the user’s identity as a contributor to something bigger, encouraging long-term engagement. The design was intentionally minimal to reduce distraction and focus attention on core values and participation pathways, fulfilling the CCI’s request for ethical, culturally sensitive, and socially connective design.

Visual and Structural Design Coherence

The Community Hub maintains alignment with the visual language used throughout the platform by using consistent typography, spacing, icon styles, and card-based containers. Each core function—guidelines, members, and announcements—is presented within a similarly styled card, creating symmetry and a balanced layout. The right sidebar complements the main content with a welcome message, a familiar sign-up structure, and an events preview, preserving the layout used in Shared Space and Events Hub. Visual hierarchy is maintained through consistent heading sizes and padding, while navigation remains accessible via the persistent sidebar and mobile menu. This coherence ensures users feel spatially and visually grounded across the site.

Accessibility and Ethical Considerations

Accessibility is carefully supported via semantic HTML5 elements, such as fieldset, legend, and proper label associations. The announcement preview and sign-up form use clear landmarks and ARIA roles to assist users relying on screen readers or keyboard navigation. All images include alt text, and form fields are validated and keyboard-accessible. Ethical design is maintained by only collecting essential personal information and avoiding commercial, manipulative, or promotional content. The structure encourages transparency and safety while prioritising inclusion, aligning with WCAG 2.2 guidelines and the ACS Code of Ethics.

Design Decisions and Refinements

Initial iterations of the Community Hub were more text-heavy and nested links within long sections. These were replaced with bold, scannable cards and clearer placements to encourage exploration. The community sign-up form was moved to a more prominent location and styled to invite immediate action. Alt text was refined for clarity, and interactive elements like dropdown menus were simplified for cleaner navigation. The card design was enhanced with consistent color application and spacing adjustments for improved touch and readability on mobile.

Accessibility Audit Summary

The IBM Equal Access Accessibility Checker confirmed WCAG 2.2 Level AA compliance. All interactive elements passed keyboard navigation checks, and screen reader testing confirmed proper element sequencing and labels. A minor adjustment was made to ensure that dropdown links within the sidebar used appropriate ARIA roles. The sign-up form’s inputs and feedback region (aria-live) were verified for dynamic updates. The audit validated that the page supports inclusive, ethical, and user-centered design across both static and interactive elements.

Community Guidlines

Alignment with Client and User Needs

The Community Guidelines page was created in response to the Connect Communities Initiative (CCI)’s directive to promote ethical, inclusive, and safe engagement across all user interactions. It fulfills a critical component of the client brief by transparently articulating behavioural standards while encouraging participation that reflects the platform’s values. Viscerally, the use of bold section markers (e.g., A–E highlights), clean spacing, and reassuring language fosters a welcoming and structured first impression. Behaviourally, the linear, scrollable layout guides users effortlessly through the content without distraction, while headings and sectional breakdowns promote scanability for readers with varying levels of digital literacy. Reflectively, the guidelines reinforce each user's role in contributing to a positive culture, reminding them of the platform’s social purpose. By using plain, inclusive language and a tone of empowerment rather than enforcement, the design invites users to internalise values rather than simply comply. The structure supports CCI’s mission to nurture diverse, respectful dialogue and ethical innovation, making this page not only a rulebook but a foundational pillar for community identity and cohesion.

Visual and Structural Design Coherence

This page uses the same typographic system, color cues, and carded layout logic established across the site, ensuring familiarity and reducing friction. Section titles are consistently styled with visual markers to signify hierarchy, while breadcrumb navigation reinforces location awareness within the Community section. The use of mark-styled initials for each guideline creates a memorable visual rhythm and reinforces structure without visual clutter. Padding, layout width, and modular “textblock” components mirror other community-facing pages, creating a unified user experience.

Accessibility and Ethical Considerations

All textual content is written in plain, accessible language, with guidelines structured using semantic HTML for screen reader support. Each block is enclosed in clearly labeled headings to support logical reading order. No unnecessary interactive distractions are present, and the mobile experience retains all critical information. From an ethical standpoint, the guidelines promote non-discrimination, transparency, and content attribution—directly echoing the ACS Code of Professional Conduct and WCAG principles. The text encourages responsible technology discourse and inclusivity, fulfilling ethical obligations outlined by the client.

Design Decisions and Refinements

The initial version of this page was paragraph-dense and less scannable. Through iteration, the content was modularised using visually distinct sections and alphabetic anchors (A–E) to improve clarity and reinforce memorability. Headings were shortened for faster comprehension, and key terms such as “inclusivity,” “privacy,” and “ethics” were emphasised for thematic consistency. The “Commitment to Community” closing section was added as a reflective gesture to elevate the tone from prescriptive to participatory, framing these rules as a mutual agreement rather than a constraint.

Minor enhancements included reinforcing focus outlines on text and ensuring ARIA live regions were isolated to the community form. After refinements, the page meets WCAG 2.2 Level AA, supporting equitable access to foundational community expectations.

Members Page

Alignment with Client and User Needs

The Members page supports the Connect Communities Initiative (CCI)’s emphasis on visibility, belonging, and connection among community participants by showcasing users who are part of the platform. In response to the client brief’s call for community celebration and shared identity, this page was designed as a space where users can see and connect with others, fostering a sense of inclusion and mutual recognition. Viscerally, the clean layout, large heading “Meet the Members!”, and spacious design establish a friendly, approachable tone, encouraging curiosity and comfort. Behaviourally, the integration of breadcrumb navigation and a clearly defined section for dynamic content (#community-list) makes the page easy to scan and prepares users to expect interactivity once profiles are loaded. Reflectively, by listing community members, the platform reinforces to users that they are not alone—that they are part of a growing network of peers with similar interests, goals, and values. This reinforces user self-worth and collective identity, aligning strongly with the CCI’s social cohesion mission. The page also gently encourages users to contribute by positioning the sign-up form alongside member discovery, making onboarding feel personal and purposeful.

Visual and Structural Design Coherence

The Members page visually aligns with the rest of the website through consistent navigation, spacing, and typography. It retains the header/sidebar/mobile navigation structure used throughout the platform and features a breadcrumb element that mirrors the Guidelines page, reinforcing hierarchy and contextual location. Headings, fieldsets, and the container for dynamic member content follow the grid and card conventions established elsewhere. The use of a right-column “Events Happening Soon” and the sign-up form further maintains consistency with Shared Space and Community Hub, offering visual continuity and predictable layout behavior.

Accessibility and Ethical Considerations

Accessibility is addressed through semantic HTML, properly labelled form inputs, and aria-live regions for real-time feedback. The breadcrumb aids in orientation for assistive technology users, and the community list is structured for screen-reader parsing once dynamically populated. All interaction points are keyboard-accessible. Ethically, the display of member data respects privacy boundaries by only showing content voluntarily provided via the sign-up form. No personal data is exposed without consent, aligning with the CCI’s ethical guidelines and the ACS Code of Professional Conduct regarding data minimisation and informed participation.

Design Decisions and Refinements

During development, the members list was initially static but evolved into a dynamically loaded section (#community-list) to reflect live participation. The heading “Meet the Members!” was chosen to be casual yet inviting, reinforcing platform friendliness. The community sign-up form was placed in the sidebar to serve as both a call to action and a contextual reinforcement of the page’s purpose. Design adjustments focused on spacing, padding, and section contrast to ensure clear visual segmentation, particularly between user actions (joining) and user discovery (browsing members).

Accessibility Audit Summary

The IBM Equal Access Accessibility Checker confirmed compliance with WCAG 2.2 Level AA standards. All labels were properly associated with input fields, and keyboard navigation across the form and breadcrumb was verified. The dynamic content area was checked for focus behavior and screen-reader support readiness. A minor improvement included clarifying the alt attribute for the event image. Overall, the Members page meets accessibility expectations, ensuring all users can engage with content and contribute equally.

Announcements Page

Alignment with Client and User Needs

The Announcements page fulfills the Connect Communities Initiative (CCI)’s brief to create an inclusive, informative platform that fosters community transparency and connection. This page acts as a central bulletin board for key updates, designed to keep users informed and engaged with minimal friction. From a visceral perspective, the page uses warm, welcoming visuals—such as a large hero image of collaboration and a human-centred layout—to immediately convey openness and positivity. Behaviourally, users can navigate the page effortlessly due to its minimalist structure, large readable text blocks, and logically ordered content hierarchy. The clear headline, publisher identity, and date stamp provide strong orientation and trust signals. The announcement content itself, structured like a personal welcome letter, encourages reflective engagement, inviting users to see themselves as part of a larger vision of creativity and innovation. This design choice was intentional in aligning with CCI’s aim to build a digital environment where users feel recognized and inspired. By integrating emotional resonance and actionable messaging, the page serves as both an onboarding tool and an ongoing engagement anchor.

Visual and Structural Design Coherence

The page’s layout mirrors other site pages in its use of grid-based alignment, font pairings, card layouts, and sidebar components. The breadcrumb-style structure of the main and article containers aligns with structural decisions seen in Shared Space and Guidelines. The visual priority is clearly given to the announcement title and primary image, maintaining content hierarchy. The announcement card shares component styling with other “carded” content across the site, ensuring visual consistency. Supporting sidebar elements—like the sign-up form and “Events Happening Soon”—maintain right-column continuity with other pages and reinforce navigational expectations.

Accessibility and Ethical Considerations

The announcement uses semantic HTML5 (article, main, fieldset) to support screen readers and assistive technologies. All form fields in the sidebar are labeled correctly, with real-time feedback provided via aria-live. Images are accompanied by descriptive alt text that communicates both content and context. Content uses simple, jargon-free language and is structured with readable paragraph spacing. Ethically, the content reinforces non-commercial, inclusive messaging, and avoids manipulation or urgency-based tactics—supporting CCI’s ethical principles and the ACS Code of Professional Conduct.

Design Decisions and Refinements

The announcement copy was drafted in a conversational tone based on best practices for community onboarding, rather than using a formal or corporate tone. This decision was based on earlier feedback indicating that users responded more positively to approachable, mission-driven content. The decision to place the “Welcome” message in a prominent article-style card allowed for a blend of editorial storytelling and functional update. The event preview and sign-up sections in the sidebar were retained to maintain actionability and help users transition from passive reading to active participation.

Accessibility Audit Summary

An IBM Equal Access Accessibility Checker audit confirmed that the Announcements page meets WCAG 2.2 Level AA compliance. All text passed contrast checks, and the main announcement content follows logical heading structure for screen reader users. Minor adjustments included adding aria-labelledby for the article title and ensuring tab focus flows to the sign-up form correctly. After testing, all user inputs and reading flows were verified as keyboard accessible and screen reader compatible, ensuring a fully inclusive and ethically aligned implementation.

Profile Page

Alignment with Client and User Needs

The Profile page was developed in response to the Connect Communities Initiative (CCI)’s objective of promoting visibility, identity, and community connection through personalised interaction. According to the client brief, the platform must celebrate individual contributions while remaining welcoming and user-centred. Viscerally, the page presents a clean, well-spaced design that feels personal and engaging through use of large avatars, user stats, and recognisable icons for social links. Behaviourally, the user is guided smoothly from static profile viewing to active interaction—such as editing their profile, viewing posts, or writing on the user wall—through clear button placement and form fields. Reflectively, this page allows users to express who they are through their posts, wall comments, and profile picture, fostering digital ownership and pride in participation. The "User Wall Comments" section in particular reflects a commitment to community building by allowing informal and ongoing dialogue, reinforcing the sense of mutual recognition. This page aligns fully with CCI’s broader mission of inclusivity and empowerment by making users feel seen, valued, and integrated into a larger collective.

Visual and Structural Design Coherence

The visual design of the Profile page follows platform-wide conventions for card layouts, typography, and icon usage. The profile information, posts, and wall comments are laid out using a grid structure consistent with other pages like Shared Space and Events Hub. The section headers ("Recent Posts", "User Wall Comments") use the same font hierarchy and spacing. Profile stats are displayed in modular units, mirroring the information block style used across the site. This coherence creates a seamless experience for users as they transition between browsing and personalisation features.

Accessibility and Ethical Considerations

All interactive elements such as buttons, inputs, and profile actions are keyboard accessible, and icons are paired with meaningful labels or roles. Images use descriptive alt text or are marked appropriately. The comment box includes placeholder text to assist users in understanding its function. The layout maintains a logical reading order, and aria attributes can be extended for improved screen reader support. Ethically, the profile page avoids revealing sensitive data by default and ensures that personalisation remains user-driven. No content is displayed unless it has been explicitly created or submitted by the user, ensuring compliance with digital rights and data transparency principles.

Design Decisions and Refinements

Initial iterations featured a more cluttered layout that grouped too many sections together. Based on feedback, the profile was refined to separate key elements—stats, recent posts, and wall comments—into clearly defined containers. Profile edit functions were simplified into a single "Edit Profile" button for future expansion. Social icons were included to support external identity linking, while the use of profile imagery and posting stats visually affirmed user participation. The comment thread layout was kept consistent with Shared Space post styling for familiarity.

Accessibility Audit Summary

The IBM Equal Access Accessibility Checker confirmed that the Profile page meets WCAG 2.2 Level AA standards. All buttons and text elements passed contrast and size requirements. The form fields and comment actions support keyboard navigation, and tab order flows logically from top to bottom. One refinement was the addition of alt text to social icons to improve non-visual interpretability. All user interactions—such as commenting or editing—are operable without a mouse, ensuring full participation regardless of input method.

Test Posts

Alignment with Client and User Needs

The Test Post page, especially in its version with embedded images, was designed to support the Connect Communities Initiative (CCI)’s mission to foster expressive, media-rich community interactions. From the client brief, there was a clear emphasis on encouraging diverse user-generated content that resonates both visually and intellectually. The page enables users to not only share their thoughts but also attach images, which aligns with both visceral design (creating instant emotional engagement through visuals) and reflective design (users feeling pride and identity through personalized, media-enhanced posts). The behavioural design aspect ensures that users can view and interact with posts and images without distraction or confusion—the image is placed within the post content in a way that enhances rather than disrupts readability. The inclusion of visual media elevates the sense of participation, particularly for visual communicators or those for whom English may not be a first language. It meets CCI’s aim to provide a platform where innovation, accessibility, and expression intersect meaningfully for all users.

Visual and Structural Design Coherence

The image-enhanced post layout maintains consistent styling with the rest of the site while enhancing richness. Images are embedded within the postcontents block, placed beneath text or between paragraphs in a non-obtrusive format. They scale responsively and are aligned to the text flow, preserving the visual balance established on other content pages. The sidebar, buttons, and metadata styling remain unchanged, ensuring that the user experience is consistent regardless of media presence.

Accessibility and Ethical Considerations

All images are required to include descriptive alt attributes to ensure screen reader accessibility and semantic understanding. The platform encourages ethical use of images by requiring users to upload only original or properly attributed content. Additionally, by placing images within a structured post format, they do not become distractions or create layout inconsistencies for users navigating with assistive technologies. Keyboard focus, alt text visibility, and non-intrusive rendering ensure equitable access across user types.

Design Decisions and Refinements

During refinement, image placement was tested in multiple positions—above, below, and within post text. The current embedded-mid-text format was chosen for its natural reading flow and improved visual hierarchy. The image size defaults to a mobile-friendly scale while allowing enlargement through future functionality (e.g., modal or lightbox). Layout padding and spacing were adjusted to ensure that the image does not crowd surrounding content or interfere with tagging and comment sections. This refinement followed design principles that prioritize clarity, scannability, and emotional impact.

Accessibility Audit Summary

The image version of the Test Post page passed all Level AA criteria of the WCAG 2.2 audit using the IBM Equal Access Checker. All images include alt text, ensuring that users with visual impairments can understand content meaningfully. Color contrast remained unaffected by image inclusion, and no keyboard traps were introduced. The layout adjusts cleanly on different devices, maintaining readable font sizes and accessible hit areas for all interactive buttons. The comment and report functionality remain fully usable via keyboard, and all image content is non-essential for navigation, ensuring graceful degradation if images do not load.
