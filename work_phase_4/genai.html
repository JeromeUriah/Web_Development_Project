GEN AI ACKNOWLEDGEMENT

1. Announcement Post Generation

Used in announcentment page

I used ChatGPT to assist in generating the initial draft of an announcement post and have adhered to the following principles. While ChatGPT generated the initial version, I reviewed and edited the content to ensure it was accurate, appropriate, and aligned with ethical communication standards relevant to the project and course expectations. The content was developed using my own understanding of the announcement’s purpose. AI was not used as a source of factual information but as a tool for drafting phrasing and structure. The AI-generated draft was refined to reflect my personal communication style, intentions, and understanding of the task. The final post represents my voice and contextual judgment. ChatGPT was selected to support the creative drafting process and to explore alternative ways to structure and phrase the announcement. It did not replace my own input, decision-making, or writing effort. AI was not used to develop content that required deep subject knowledge or critical analysis. Its role was limited to generating language-based suggestions for the announcement layout and tone. No personal, private, or sensitive data was included in any AI prompt. All input related solely to general aspects of the announcement’s content and tone. I have reviewed and followed the GenAI and MT Usage Framework and the course profile’s expectations for responsible AI use in assessments.

3. Picture Generation

Used in everypage

I used Samsung Sketch’s image generative AI feature to support a two-stage creative process in this task, beginning with the manual creation of a base drawing and then transforming it using the sketch-to-image function. In accordance with the GenAI and MT Usage Framework and the expectations outlined in the course profile, I ensured that the AI was used as a tool for creative augmentation rather than a replacement for personal artistic effort. The visual output was critically reviewed for unintended stylistic bias, acknowledging the potential influence of training data on generative results, even though overt bias was not immediately apparent. Ethical use was maintained by ensuring that the starting point was my own original sketch, allowing the AI transformation to serve as an interpretive layer rather than an autonomous creation. While image generation does not rely on factual sources, I assessed the visual coherence and quality of the AI-generated image against established design principles and personal artistic standards. The iterative process of producing and refining the image preserved my own understanding and artistic intent, as the AI’s output was guided and constrained by my initial drawing. The tool was selected specifically to explore how generative AI can enhance rather than bypass the creative process, reinforcing the course’s emphasis on using GenAI to support learning and originality. At no point did this process involve the input of personal or sensitive data. This use of AI was fully compliant with course requirements, transparently acknowledged in this reflection, and consistent with responsible academic and professional practice.
